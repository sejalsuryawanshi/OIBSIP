A.P.J.Abdul Kalam

The missile man of India.

Here's a time line of A.P.J.Abdul Kalam:

  • 1931 - Born in Rameshwaram, India
  • 1954 - He completed his schooling from Ramanathapuram Schwartz Matriculation School and proceeded to study physics at the Saint Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli, from where he graduated."
  • 1960 - He studied aerospace engineering in Madras Institute of Technology, graduating in 1960.
  • 1965 - An expandable rocket project on which he had started working independently."
  • 1969 - Kalam was transferred to the Indian Space Research Organisation.
  • 1970 - He worked on the development of ballistic missiles from the technology of the successful SLV program and directed the projects Project Devil and Project Valiant which were aimed at producing a short-range surface-to-air missile.
  • 1980 - The government decided to initiate an advanced missile program under his directorship and thus the Integrated Guided Missile Development Program (IGMDP) was launched with Kalam as the chief executive.
  • 1981 - A. P. J. Abdul Kalam has been honored with several prestigious awards from the Government of India including the Padma Bhushan.
  • 1982 - The program which aimed at the research and development of a comprehensive range of missiles started
  • 1990 - Honored with Padma Vibhushan.
  • 1997 - Honored with Bharat Ratna.
  • 2002 - Kalam stood for presidency and easily won the 2002 presidential election.
  • 2005 - He kindled controversy with his decision to impose President's Rule in Bihar.
  • 2007 - He decided not to contest the Presidential election again and stepped down.
  • 2013 - He was the recipient of the Von Braun Award (2013) from the National Space Society "to recognize excellence in the management and leadership of a space-related project".
  • 2015 - Died of a cardiac arrest .

    • "Abdul Kalam was a mortal human being just like us, but for his contribution to the country he remained immortal in the hearts of people."
